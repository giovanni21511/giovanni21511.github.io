Biotech Breakthroughs

In our science class, we did a cool experiment to learn about DNA. We used a toothpick to scrub inside our cheeks to get a tiny bit of our DNA. Then we mixed it with special liquid in a tube. After that, we put the tubes in a machine that helps us see our DNA. I learned that even though people might look different on the outside, our DNA can be very similar to people from all over the world.

The skill I got way better at was being a critical thinker. It was hard work to look at our results and think about what they meant. I had to pay close attention to the details and not just guess what the answers were. I really had to think about how science works and why my results were different than what I first thought.

Is Race "Real"?

Biological Race

Claim: Race is not biologically real.

Evidence: Our DNA testing showed that people of the same race showed no DNA relation, but people of different races shared DNA and matched up with one another.

Reasoning: I believe that race is not biologically proven and that it’s more of a social construct. If race was biologically real, then in our DNA testing experiment, we would have seen multiple matches of people that were the same race and people of different races wouldn't have matched up at all.

Social & Psychological Race

Claim: Race is socially and psychologically real.

Evidence: We learned about the Implicit Association Test. It measures how people feel about others inside their heads, even if they don't mean to.

Reasoning: Even if it isn't real in biology, race is real in how people treat each other. It is like a moving walkway that carries people in different directions based on how they look.
